About Bill Gates's

Bill Gates is the entrepreneur responsible for founding Microsoft - the world's best known software company. He is also recognized as one of the pioneers in the computer revolution aimed at the common consumer.

William Henry Gates III was born in the city of Seattle, in the United States, in 1955. At the age of 17, Gates and Paul Allen developed software for reading magnetic tapes: his first company, Traf-o-Data, was born. However, the deal did not go ahead due to the low age of the partners, which did not demonstrate credibility.

In 1973, Bill Gates joined Harvard University. At the institution, he studied mathematics and law, but dropped out about two years later.

In partnership with the former partner, he created the BASIC language interpretation system, aimed at the Altair 8800 computer.

With the money earned from the sale of the project, Bill Gates and Paul Allen founded Microsoft - created to develop software for PCs.

The big rise came in 1977, when the computer giant at the time, IBM, invested in the launch of personal computers.

At the time, they closed a deal with Microsoft that bought a program designed for the Intel processor, for $ 50,000. After customizing gagdet, Microsoft resold it for $ 8 million, while maintaining the product license. It was MS-DOS.

In the early 1980s, Microsoft launched Windows - which gradually occupied most computers. At just over 30, Bill Gates was already a billionaire.

In the early part of the 2000s, he announced that he would gradually move away from Microsoft's day-to-day. Although it did this in 2008, it still influences the company's main decisions.

Engaged in philanthropic causes, Bill Gates founded in 2000, alongside his wife, Melinda, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. The organization invests in research that seeks to cure AIDS and some other diseases that mainly plague underdeveloped countries. In addition, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation has part of the resources destined to research new types of clean and sustainable energies.

In 2017 alone, Bill Gates donated 5% of his fortune in Microsoft shares to charities - most of which went to the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. There were 64 million shares - at the date, the estimated value was US $ 4.6 billion. According to Bloomberg's calculation, before that he had donated US $ 35 billion to social actions over the years.

The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation was responsible for creating the Giving Pledge which, alongside Warren Buffett, mobilized as many billionaires to donate most of their fortunes to charity.
